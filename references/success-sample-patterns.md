# Success Sample Patterns

Use this reference to match an approved house style. It is distilled from an anonymized successful WeChat article. Learn the structure, editorial stance, paragraph rhythm, and rigor/readability balance; do not reuse the sample's facts, topic, or distinctive wording mechanically.

## Core Pattern

The approved sample works because it is not a translated article with a short introduction. It is an editor-framed compilation:

1. Start with an `编辑说明` that tells readers why this Japanese source matters.
2. State the top-level thesis before entering the source material.
3. Break the topic into mutually distinct parts.
4. Make each part answer one clear question.
5. Make each paragraph start with a controlling idea, then add explanation, example, or implication.
6. End by returning to the opening thesis at a higher level of abstraction.

This creates "总-分" at three levels: article, section, and paragraph.

## Opening Architecture

Use the opening to establish the reading lens before details appear.

An effective opening should usually do five jobs:

- Name the macro context or current debate.
- Contrast the Chinese reading context with the Japanese perspective, without stereotyping either side.
- Explain what is distinctive about the Japanese angle.
- State why this source was selected.
- Tell the reader what conceptual frame to use while reading.

The successful sample's opening does this by moving from the global AI wave, to differences between Chinese and Japanese discussion habits, to Japan's concern with boundary, condition, and responsibility. For future articles, reproduce that logic, not the exact wording.

Useful opening move:

- "这篇文章真正值得读的地方，不是它提供了一个简单结论，而是它把一个被热烈讨论的问题重新拆成了几个更可判断的前提。"

## Source Metadata Placement

After the editorial framing, provide concise source context when it helps authority and transparency:

- original title
- author or institution
- publication outlet or publisher
- publication date if available
- author/institution credentials relevant to the topic

Keep metadata short. Its job is to establish credibility, not interrupt the narrative.

## MECE Part Design

Each major part should cover one dimension only. Do not organize mechanically by the source paragraph order.

Define 2-4 parts that are collectively sufficient and mutually distinct. Examples:

- policy design: problem definition / institutional tool / implementation constraint / evaluation risk
- industrial strategy: market pressure / technology route / supply-chain position / policy signal
- research commercialization: scientific result / translation mechanism / funding or governance bottleneck / social implication
- regional innovation: local pain point / actor network / resource allocation / replicability limit

## Section Heading Style

Headings should carry argument or tension, not just label a topic.

Preferred heading forms:

- question plus answer direction: "为什么……仍然困难"
- contrast pair: "能抽取信息，却难把握意图"
- metaphor plus mechanism: "看似合理的错答，会成为商业风险"
- named concept plus plain-language explanation: "RAG：先查资料，再生成答案"
- quote-like hook plus analytic question: "‘那位负责人就喜欢这种’能否被形式化？"

Avoid vague headings such as "背景介绍", "原因分析", "启示意义" unless they are sharpened by concrete content.

## Paragraph Pattern

Most paragraphs should follow this order:

1. topic sentence: give the paragraph's main judgment
2. mechanism: explain why this is true
3. example or consequence: make it concrete
4. optional bridge: connect to the next paragraph

Use short mobile-friendly paragraphs. A paragraph can be only one sentence when it performs a transition or emphasis function.

Good paragraph moves:

- "也就是说，……"
- "问题在于，……"
- "如果把这一点放回……场景，就会发现……"
- "这并不是……，而是……"
- "从流程设计的角度看，……；但如果进一步追问……，就必须……"

Use these as structural moves, not as repeated filler.

## Making Serious Content Interesting

The sample is readable because it names abstract problems with memorable but controlled expressions. Use compact conceptual handles to make mechanisms legible.

For future drafts:

- Give each major problem a compact conceptual handle.
- Use examples from work, policy, research, company operations, or everyday decision-making.
- Let examples clarify the mechanism, not replace analysis.
- Keep the tone curious and measured.
- Use contrast to create momentum: "不是能不能做，而是在什么条件下可以做、到哪一步必须停手。"

Do not use entertainment-account hooks, sensational adjectives, or forced jokes.

## Think-Tank Rigor

The writing can be engaging, but every engaging sentence should still be accountable to a concept, mechanism, source fact, or editorial inference.

Maintain rigor by:

- preserving source facts and uncertainty
- explaining causal links instead of only listing observations
- identifying conditions and limits
- using "可能", "往往", "在……场景中", "从……角度看" when claims are conditional
- avoiding broad "对中国的启示" unless the mechanism is specified

## Conclusion Pattern

The conclusion should not merely summarize. It should return to the opening lens and slightly deepen it.

Useful closing arc:

1. Restate what the case/article shows.
2. Clarify the boundary, condition, mechanism, or unresolved question.
3. Offer a restrained reflection for Chinese policy or research readers.
4. End with a calm, memorable sentence rather than a slogan.

Close by naming the most useful distinction the reader can carry away.

## Editing Checklist

Before finalizing a draft in this house style, check:

- The opening gives a clear total thesis before detailed sections.
- Each major part answers a different question.
- Each section begins with the point, then explains.
- Most paragraphs begin with a controlling idea.
- Examples are concrete and tied back to the mechanism.
- The Japanese perspective remains visible.
- The prose has rhythm, but not hype.
- Any repeated or near-repeated paragraphs are merged.
- The ending returns to the opening frame and raises the level of insight.
